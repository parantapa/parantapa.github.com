Hello World!

My first post on my blog. A few days back I was searching for a decent x86_64/amd64 and AT&T assembly “hello world” program to kickstart my assembly programing quest. I didn’t find anything that I really liked. So here is my version.

milw0rm.com/papers/110 this is the webpage that got me of doing what I wanted to do. It requires you to know basic x86 assembly and system call primitives of Linux.

/*
* hello.s
*/
    .section .rodata
buf:
    .string "hello, world\n"
    .set count, . - buf

    .section .text
    .globl _start
_start:
    movq    $1, %rax
    movq    $1, %rdi
    movq    $buf, %rsi
    movq    $count, %rdx
    syscall

    movq    $60, %rax
    movq    $0, %rdi
    syscall

Compile it as follows:

$ gcc hello.s -o hello -nostdib
